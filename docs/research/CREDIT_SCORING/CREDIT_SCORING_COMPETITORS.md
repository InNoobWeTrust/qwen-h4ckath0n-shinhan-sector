## VERIFIED FACTS:

- Vietnamese SME lending is already moving beyond classic collateral-only underwriting. A 2025 `Vietnam News` report says Resolution 68 pushes banks to expand unsecured business lending using cash-flow management, business performance reports, VAT declarations, e-invoices, and API links with accounting software, tax authorities, and CIC. The same report says `BIDV`, `Techcombank`, and `MB Bank` partnered with `MISA` on `MISA Lending` for digital SME loans.
- `Techcombank` publicly markets its `MISA Lending` product for MSME/SME customers using MISA software, with automated pre-approval in 5 minutes and digital use of existing financial data. This is clear evidence that at least some Vietnamese banks are using accounting/e-invoice/cash-flow data for SME credit decisions.
- `MISA Lending` publicly lists multiple lending partners in Vietnam, including `BIDV`, `MB`, `Techcombank`, `Vietcombank`, `VPBank`, `Sacombank`, `Standard Chartered`, `EVNFC`, `VietCredit`, and `Cake`. That means the market already has a live platform layer connecting SME operating data to lenders.
- `Shopee` launched an official Vietnam seller-loan product: `SEasy Cho Vay Người Bán`. Shopee's seller education page says it is available to Shopee sellers and ShopeeFood merchant partners, is provided via credit-institution partners through the Shopee platform, can be appraised within 24 hours, and names `TPBank` as a lending partner on the page.
- `Validus Vietnam` is an active SME lender in Vietnam, not just a generic fintech brand. Its site advertises unsecured SME financing, automated credit assessment, disbursement within 48 hours, and a specific partnership with `Haravan` for online SME and household-business loans.
- `Techcombank` already has merchant infrastructure products in market: `MerchantOne`, `SoftPOS`, and `ShopCash`. `SoftPOS` and `MerchantOne` show Techcombank is collecting/serving merchant payment flows; `ShopCash` shows Techcombank already lends unsecured to merchants/household businesses.
- `MoMo` publicly says it has an `AI Credit Scoring` capability. On MoMo's careers site, the company says its scoring system uses transaction and behavioral data from the MoMo ecosystem to assess creditworthiness. A 2025 article republished by `Vietnam.vn` from `Sai Gon Giai Phong` says MoMo's merchant-oriented digital stack can use real transaction data to generate credit scores that help small merchants access unsecured bank loans.
- `Findo` and `FE Credit` are real lenders in Vietnam, but their official sites show consumer-finance products, not SME underwriting products. `Findo` is personal online cash lending; `FE Credit` is consumer cash loans, instalment finance, and credit cards.

## MARKET GAPS:

- I found no verified public evidence that Vietnamese banks are explicitly underwriting SME loans from `POS transaction data` in the narrow sense of card-terminal/SoftPOS sales histories. Banks clearly have merchant payments products, but public product pages do not say those POS flows are the credit input.
- I found no verified public evidence that Vietnamese banks are directly using `MoMo` or `ZaloPay` wallet transaction feeds as a disclosed SME credit-decision input. MoMo talks about transaction-based scoring inside its own ecosystem, but bank-side disclosure is still weak.
- I found a verified live seller-loan product for `Shopee`, but I did **not** find equally strong public evidence for a live Vietnam seller-lending product from `Lazada` or `TikTok Shop`.
- The named lenders `Findo`, `FE Credit`, and likely `Mirae Asset` are mostly consumer lenders, so they do not meaningfully close the SME alternative-data gap.
- Public evidence is much stronger for `accounting software + e-invoice + tax/cash-flow` underwriting than for `marketplace sales + POS data + e-wallet cash flow` underwriting.

## COMPETITOR PRODUCTS:

- `MISA Lending`: bank marketplace for SME digital loans using business operating data; partners include `BIDV`, `MB`, `Techcombank`, and others.
- `Shopee SEasy Cho Vay Người Bán`: official seller financing product in Vietnam for Shopee sellers and ShopeeFood merchants, with `TPBank` named on Shopee's page.
- `Validus Vietnam`: unsecured SME financing, supply-chain financing, and `eBIZ` digital loans; partnership with `Haravan` is especially relevant because it connects commerce software/platform activity to lending.
- `Techcombank ShopCash / MerchantOne / SoftPOS`: not a clean "POS-data credit scoring" product, but an important adjacent stack combining merchant acceptance, merchant operations, and unsecured lending.
- `MoMo` merchant ecosystem + `AI Credit Scoring`: relevant as a data layer and scoring capability, but the public proof is stronger for consumer/merchant ecosystem scoring than for a fully disclosed bank SME underwriting product.
- `Findo` and `FE Credit`: existing lenders in Vietnam, but focused on consumer finance rather than SME cash-flow underwriting.

## THE ACTUAL EDGE (if any):

- The market is **not empty**. Vietnam already has live products using alternative business data for SME lending, especially through `MISA Lending`, supply-chain finance, and marketplace-embedded seller loans.
- The real white space is narrower: a lender that can underwrite SMEs from **multi-source live operating data** across `POS`, `marketplace sales`, `e-wallet inflows/outflows`, `e-invoices`, and `accounting data` in one model, rather than relying on one captive ecosystem.
- Another real gap is `cross-platform data portability`. Existing products are mostly tied to one channel (`Shopee`), one software stack (`MISA`), or one financing ecosystem (`Validus` partnerships). A lender that normalizes data across channels may still be differentiated.
- A bank-grade product that publicly proves: `seller data + payment data + wallet cash-flow -> underwriting -> fast loan approval` is still hard to find in Vietnam. That is the strongest evidence of remaining room in the market.
- If positioning this opportunity honestly: the new angle is **not** "Vietnam has no alternative-data SME lending." The stronger angle is "Vietnam has partial solutions, but cross-channel underwriting from POS + marketplace + wallet cash flow still appears under-disclosed and under-productized."
