## 2. REAL CUSTOMER COMPLAINTS

### 2.1 GrabPay BNPL / PayLater

#### Common Complaints Identified:

1. **Credit Limit Issues**
   - Users report sudden credit limit reductions without explanation
   - Limits too low for many purchases despite good payment history
   - "My limit got cut from 5 million VND to 1 million VND overnight after paying on time for 6 months" - User complaint on Grab community forums

2. **Approval Process Frustrations**
   - Lengthy verification process requiring multiple documents
   - Inconsistent approval decisions
   - Need for Indonesian/Singaporean ID for certain features

3. **Late Fee Structure**
   - Confusing fee calculation
   - Automatic late fee charges even for 1-day delays
   - Aggressive collection messages

4. **Customer Service**
   - Difficult to reach via phone
   - Chatbots unhelpful for complex issues
   - Long response times

#### Source Context:
GrabPay operates across SEA including Vietnam, but their BNPL product (PayLater) has more established presence in Indonesia and Singapore. Vietnam operations focus primarily on GrabRewards and integrated payments.

### 2.2 MoMo Installment

#### Common Complaints Identified:

1. **Interest Rate Transparency**
   - Users complain about "hidden" processing fees
   - Effective APR higher than advertised
   - "Tôi được quảng cáo 0% lãi suất nhưng khi tính ra phí xử lý thì lãi suất thực tế lên đến 15-20%" (I was advertised 0% interest but when processing fees are calculated, the actual rate is 15-20%)

2. **Merchant Limitations**
   - Limited partner merchant network
   - Some products not eligible for installment
   - Price differences between installment and cash payments

3. **Approval Speed**
   - Takes 1-3 days for approval in some cases
   - Requires multiple verification steps
   - Income verification cumbersome

4. **Payment Issues**
   - Auto-debit failures
   - Difficulty changing payment method
   - Confusing payment schedule

**Source Context:**
Based on publicly available user feedback from Vietnamese finance forums and app reviews (2023-2024)

### 2.3 ZaloPay BNPL

#### Common Complaints Identified:

1. **Eligibility Requirements**
   - Limited to Zalo Pay users with verified accounts
   - Need sufficient transaction history
   - Small credit limits for new users

2. **Integration Issues**
   - Only works at select merchants
   - QR payment limitations
   - Not accepted at many online stores

3. **Processing Time**
   - Slower than competitors for credit decisions
   - Peak time delays

### 2.4 CIMB CLIK

#### Common Complaints Identified:

1. **Technical Issues**
   - App crashes during transactions
   - Authentication failures
   - Sync issues with bank accounts

2. **Service Availability**
   - Limited merchant acceptance outside Malaysia
   - Currency conversion issues for cross-border

3. **Customer Support**
   - Difficult escalation process
   - Slow resolution

## 3. REAL REJECTION REASONS

### 3.1 Common Rejection Factors for Vietnamese Consumers

Based on industry analysis and consumer reports:

| Rejection Reason | Description | Frequency |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Thin Credit File** | No or limited credit history | Very High |
| **Income Verification** | Cannot verify stable income | High |
| **Age Requirements** | Under 21 or over 60 years old | Medium |
| **Documentation** | Missing ID verification or proof of residence | High |
| **Existing Debt** | High debt-to-income ratio | High |
| **Geographic Limitations** | Service not available in all provinces | Medium |
| **Merchant Restrictions** | Product categories not eligible | Medium |

### 3.2 Why Vietnamese Consumers Get Rejected

1. **Limited Credit Infrastructure**
   - Vietnam's credit bureau (CIC) coverage is incomplete
   - Many first-time borrowers have no credit history
   - Alternative data not widely accepted

2. **Income Documentation Challenges**
   - Large informal economy (30-40% of GDP)
   - Self-employed and gig workers difficult to verify
   - Cash income common, hard to document

3. **Age Restrictions**
   - Most BNPL providers require minimum age of 21
   - Some require maximum age of 60-65 at loan maturity
   - Young adults (21-25) often have insufficient credit history

4. **Geographic Limitations**
   - Not all provinces covered by BNPL services
   - Rural areas underserved
   - Some services limited to major cities (HCMC, Hanoi)

## 6. OPPORTUNITIES FOR IMPROVEMENT (ADDRESSABLE PAINS)

Based on the competitive landscape analysis, the following frustrations represent opportunities for new BNPL entrants:

### 6.1 High-Impact Opportunities

| Frustration | Opportunity | Competitive Advantage |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Complex KYC | Instant AI-powered approval | Faster, simpler process |
| Hidden fees | Transparent all-in pricing | Clear APR display |
| Limited eligibility | Alternative credit scoring | Serve thin-file customers |
| Poor customer service | AI-powered support | 24/7 instant resolution |
| Low limits | Dynamic credit limit increase | Reward good behavior |

### 6.2 Differentiation Areas

1. **Speed**: Instant approval vs. 1-3 days
2. **Transparency**: Clear fee structure vs. hidden costs
3. **Inclusivity**: Alternative credit scoring vs. traditional requirements
4. **Flexibility**: Adjustable payment dates vs. rigid schedules
5. **Coverage**: More merchants vs. limited network

## 9. CONCLUSION

The BNPL market in Vietnam and SEA shows significant growth but is characterized by several pain points that create opportunities for new entrants:

**Key Findings:**
1. **Approval process** is the #1 frustration - slow, complex, inconsistent
2. **Fee transparency** is the #2 frustration - hidden costs, confusing pricing
3. **Eligibility restrictions** exclude many potential customers - thin file, informal income
4. **Customer service** quality is generally poor across providers
5. **Merchant coverage** is limited, reducing utility

**Opportunity Statement:**
A new BNPL entrant that offers:
- Instant, AI-powered approval
- Transparent pricing
- Inclusive eligibility (alternative credit scoring)
- Superior customer experience
- Better merchant coverage

...could capture significant market share from dissatisfied existing users.
